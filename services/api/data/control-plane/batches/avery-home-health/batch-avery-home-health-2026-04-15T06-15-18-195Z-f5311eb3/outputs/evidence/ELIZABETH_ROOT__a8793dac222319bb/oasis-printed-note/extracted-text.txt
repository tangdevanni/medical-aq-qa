exertional lower-extremity pain with ambulation, and requires a power chair for intra-home mobility (current power chair is too large for safe use at home and she is in the process of obtaining
an appropriately sized chair). She is able to transfer but has very limited ability to ambulate and is at high fall risk due to extreme obesity, knee osteoarthritis, lymphedema, neuropathic pain, and
deconditioning. The patient lives alone with no live-in caregiver (friend provides near-daily assistance), which increases safety risk if she were to leave home and require unassisted ambulation
or recovery from exertion. Leaving the home requires a considerable and taxing effort because even minimal ambulation provokes dyspnea and lower-extremity pain, she can only take a few
steps and cannot sustain standing long enough to perform transfers or traverse typical community distances, and her current mobility equipment is inadequate for safe community ambulation.
Exiting the home would therefore require either multiple persons and/or specialized equipment (appropriately sized power chair and transport) and poses significant fall and respiratory risk.
Allowable, limited absences are restricted to medically necessary appointments or brief tolerated activities with appropriate transport and assistance; prolonged or frequent community outings
would exceed her functional tolerance and increase risk of decompensation. Given these documented impairments (morbid obesity, bilateral knee osteoarthritis, bilateral lower-extremity
lymphedema with dependent edema, chronic hypoxic respiratory failure/COPD, CKD stage 3, painful foot calluses, inability to ambulate more than a few steps, requirement for power mobility,
and lack of a live-in caregiver), skilled home health services are medically necessary to provide skilled nursing for medication adjustment and renal monitoring after diuretic change, supervised
management of oxygen therapy and respiratory symptoms, and skilled PT/OT for strengthening, gait/transfer training, power-chair assessment and training, and fall-risk mitigation, as well as
