Primary Reason for Home Health/Medical Necessity (POC Element):
Nancy Vu is a 90-year-old female patient admitted to home health care due to end-stage renal disease requiring intermittent hemodialysis, chronic oxygen dependence at home, baseline
dementia and is reported bedbound at home with family caretakers. The patient is considered homebound because she was brought by EMS after being found with a room air saturation <50%
requiring BVM/NRB and subsequent continuous BiPAP and stat hemodialysis for hyperkalemia and volume overload. Objective findings supporting homebound status include: acute on chronic
hypoxic respiratory failure, CT showing bilateral interlobular septal thickening/ground-glass opacities with small-moderate pleural effusions, documented Morganella morganii bacteremia (blood
culture +), ongoing IV antibiotic therapy, anemia, troponinemia consistent with demand ischemia, and eGFR/creatinine consistent with ESRD. Functional and safety limitations clearly
documented in PT/OT/SLP evaluations make independent community departure unsafe: PT/OT note dependence for all ADLs and mobility, use of a manual wheelchair at home, bilateral
lower-extremity weakness, and need for 1:1 supervision for feeding due to visual impairment and cognitive deficits.
Leaving the home would require multiple skilled interventions and substantial, taxing effort, transfer and mobilization with trained caregivers, transport while managing supplemental oxygen and
IV antibiotic/dialysis access, and real-time clinical monitoring for hypoxia, hemodynamic instability, electrolyte shifts, and bacteremia, all of which will increase risk of decompensation if
attempted without skilled support. Allowable, limited absences are recognized and should be restricted to medically necessary trips with appropriate skilled escort and monitoring; other brief,
supervised outings only as tolerated.
Given the combination of recent critical respiratory compromise requiring respiratory support, ongoing need for dialysis via an indwelling catheter, active bacteremia on antibiotics, significant
mobility dependence with mechanical lift transfers, dementia with feeding supervision needs, and anemia/troponinemia requiring medical monitoring, these objective findings render the patient
substantially unable to leave home without considerable and taxing effort and establish the medical necessity for skilled home health services to ensure safe care and prevent readmission.
