Home Health Protocol
Dr. Khavkin uses two different techniques to close the wound, depending on the patient's skin
and overall body type.
1. The most common type of closure involves subcutaneous absorbable sutures with the
skin closed with the glue (Dermabond) and a Steri-strip that is attached to the incision.
On top of this, he puts a piece of Telfa dressing covered with a tape.
The tape and Telfa can come off two days after the surgery. The glue and the Steri-Strips
are waterproof and it's ok for the patient to take shower but not a bath or pools. The
Steri-Strips usually fall off about two weeks after the surgery or Dr. Khavkin removes it
when he sees the patient for a follow-up appointment 2 weeks after the surgery. Do not
remove Stril Strip by yourself.
2. The second type of closure is when he uses staples, which then are covered with the
antibiotic ointment and the Telfa dressing with the tape on top of it. With this kind of
closure, the patient should not be taking shower without the incision being covered till
the follow up appointment. The dressing needs to be replaced by Home Health Care
team during each visit.
At any point, if the HHC team member sees any concerns with the wound healing such
as redness, swelling, opening of the wound or any kind of discharge, Dr.Khavkin and his
staff need to be notified immediately.
You should also notify Dr. Khavkin's staff if the patient has persistent pain, difficulty
swallowing, signs of infection or any numbness, tingling or weakness in the extremities.
You can start gentle physical therapy on the extremities without working on the area of
the surgery (neck or back). Dr. Khavkin usually starts formal outpatient physical therapy
around 4-6 weeks after the surgery.
