DESCRIPTION OF PROCEDURE: The patient was identified in the preoperative holding area where
the correct patient, laterality, and procedure were confirmed. The risks and benefits of surgery were
discussed and I marked the left wrist with my initials. The patient was brought to the operative suite
where she was administered general anesthesia. A non-sterile tourniquet was placed on the left upper
extremity. The left upper extremity was prepped and draped in a standard sterile fashion. A brief
time-out was performed to verify the correct patient, laterality, and procedure. All were in agreement.
An Esmarch was used and the tourniquet was inflated to 230 mmHg. I started with the volar mass. I
made a longitudinal incision along the FCR and then made another incision along the plane of the thenar
muscle centered over the mass. I incised the dermis. I spread and identified several small arterial
branches which were cauterized and mobilized. I identified the mass. I traced it down to the radiocarpal
joint capsule. I cauterized it at the stalk and sent it as a specimen to Pathology. I then explored and found
no residual mass. I irrigated the area with curette and used a 3-0 Vicryl suture to sew up the hole. This
completed my left volar mass excision.
Then, I turned my attention to the dorsal wrist/hand. There was a large swelling and protuberance over
the dorsal hand. I made a large longitudinal incision over the hand. I incised the dermis. I spread down.
There was large protuberant mass which was encompassing all the extensor tendons of the fourth
compartment. I isolated the tendons initially to the index, middle, ring, and small. The ring and small finger
tendons were completely ruptured in an attritional manner with small fibrous bands bridging the tendon.
The mass was very inflamed, protuberant tenosynovitis with gross rice bodies. As much of this was
debulked and removed as possible, I completed the tenosynovectomy of the index, middle, ring, and small
fingers. This was sent as a composite specimen to Pathology. The index and middle finger tendons were
frayed but were intact. The ring and small fingers were not. I resected the bridging synovial bands. I
trimmed and freshened up the edges of the ring and small finger tendons. I then used 4-0 MaxBraid
suture and did a four-portion repair to repair the remaining tendons back to one another. The remainder of
the bulkiness was inflamed synovium. In order to remove this, I would have to remove the capsule and I
did not feel safe or comfortable doing this. At this point, I determined I had to remove as much of the
mass in the form of tenosynovitis as I could safely do with this approach. The tourniquet was down.
