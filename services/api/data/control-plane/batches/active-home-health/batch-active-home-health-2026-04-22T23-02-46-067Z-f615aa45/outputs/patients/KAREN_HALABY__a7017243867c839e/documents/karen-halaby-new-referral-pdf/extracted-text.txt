Plan: Counseling - Orthobiologics.
Orthobiologics refers to products such as tissue, cells, and molecules made by those cells called growth factors that may aid in
tissue healing and regeneration.
Autologous platelet rich plasma (PRP) is the component of blood that has been prepared to maximize the concentration of platelets
and their associated growth factors. The PRP can be introduced into the painful joint or soft tissue to wake up the signaling
between these molecules necessary for healing. Autologous means that your own cells are used, eliminating much of the concern
regarding disease transmission or allergic reaction. PRP can be obtained from a standard blood draw. There is evidence to support
the potential benefits of PRP for some tendon and joint disorders.
Some growth factors are used in orthopedic surgery to aid the healing of bone and soft tissue structures.
Stem cells are cells that have the potential to turn into different types, or lineages, of cells, such as the cells that make up muscle,
tendon, cartilage, and bone. Stem cells produce and release molecules called growth factors that participate in communication
between cells. The richest source of stem cells in the body is the bone marrow. There are basic science studies that support the
ability of stem cells to help with healing and repair. There is ongoing research to establish the efficacy of stem cell delivery at the
site of musculoskeletal injury.
PRP therapy starts with drawing your blood and placing the whole blood volume in a centrifuge, which serves to separate the
desired PRP cells into a richly concentrated layer. You will then be positioned to allow access of the joint or area to be injected.
After sterile preparation of the area, the PRP is delivered by injection. Ultrasound is sometimes used to target the exact location for
treatment. PRP can also be administered at the time of surgery. PRP can be planned as a single injection or a series of injections.
